En Fi

We've gotten all the parts and components into a pc - what's next? Let's go through some common issues and their solutions together.

__

Compatibility Issues

Problem: The processor and motherboard don't match, the RAM is the wrong type or speed, the graphics card doesn't fit in the case, or the power supply is underpowered.

Solution:

  • Use the PCPartPicker website to check part compatibility.
  • Check the CPU socket, RAM type, and GPU dimensions on manufacturers' websites.
  • Choose a power supply with 20–30% more wattage than your system's estimated power draw.
__

POST (Power-On Self-Test) Failures

Problem: The PC won't boot or show a display due to poorly seated components or missing power cables.

Solution:

  • Reseat the RAM, GPU, and CPU carefully.
  • Ensure the 24-pin motherboard power cable and 8-pin CPU power cable are securely connected.
  • Try booting with a minimal hardware setup (1 RAM stick, CPU, GPU if required).
  • Clear the CMOS settings and use beep codes or motherboard LEDs for troubleshooting.
__

Cooling and Airflow Issues

Problem: Overheating or poor airflow due to incorrectly installed fans or missing thermal paste.

Solution:

  • Install fans correctly: intake at the front and bottom, exhaust at the back and top.
  • Use a pea-sized amount of thermal paste when mounting the CPU cooler.
  • Choose a cooler suitable for your CPU’s TDP (thermal design power).
  • Keep cables out of airflow paths to maintain good ventilation.
__

Cable Management and Connections

Problem: Loose or incorrectly connected cables, especially front panel connectors and SATA cables.

Solution:

  • Refer to the motherboard manual for correct front panel pin connections.
  • Route cables behind the motherboard tray and secure them with zip ties.
  • Make sure SATA and power cables are firmly connected to devices and the motherboard.
__

BIOS/UEFI Issues

Problem: Outdated BIOS, wrong boot drive selected, or RAM not running at full speed.

Solution:

  • Update the BIOS if using a newer CPU model.
  • Set the correct boot drive in BIOS settings.
  • Enable the XMP/DOCP profile so RAM runs at its rated speed.
__

Static Electricity

Problem: Damaging components due to electrostatic discharge.

Solution:

  • Use an anti-static wrist strap or regularly touch a metal surface.
  • Build your PC on a hard surface, not on carpet.
  • Handle components by the edges, avoiding connectors and circuitry.
__

Driver and Operating System Installation Issues

Problem: The operating system doesn't recognize drives, or network/display drivers are missing.

Solution:

  • Download network and GPU drivers in advance to a USB stick.
  • Create a bootable USB installer with the Windows Media Creation Tool.
  • Delete old partitions and let Windows reformat the drive cleanly.
__

Wrong or Missing Parts

Problem: Ordering incompatible parts or forgetting essential components.

Solution:

  • Double-check your part list using PCPartPicker.
  • Buy parts from reputable sellers that offer return policies.
  • Make a checklist before starting: CPU, GPU, RAM, PSU, cooler, storage, case, cables, operating system.
